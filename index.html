The Rawalpindi Ring Road

The Rawalpindi Ring Road is a 57-kilometre (35 mi) long ring road around the city of Rawalpindi in Pakistan. It was constructed in 2007 to alleviate traffic congestion in the city.The road is divided into six sections, each of which is named after a different landmark in the city. The sections are as follows:

  • N-5 National Highway Section
  • Rawalpindi Cantonment Section
  • Rawalpindi Saddar Section
  • Rawalpindi-Islamabad Motorway (M-1) Section
  • GT Road Section
  • Rawalpindi-Murree Expressway (N-75) Section

The Rawalpindi Ring Road is a vital transportation artery for the city and its surrounding areas. It has helped to reduce traffic congestion and improve travel times. The road is also a major tourist attraction, as it offers stunning views of the city and its surroundings.
